Expression 2 Raytracer

  I'll cut the philosophy and cut to the trace—this thing was an absolutely terrible idea in retrospect. Who would be such an idiot as to write a rendering engine that runs SERVERSIDE, in a language that's effectively interpreted TWICE? Yup, it was me. That said, I've learned a lot over the two years I've been developing this tracer over—mostly how O(n) in practice feels a lot longer than you expect, and how anything that takes O(n^2) or longer is a disgrace to computer scientists everywhere. And yes, I realize that includes my entire tracer since the screen is n by n. This thing should never have even been allowed to exist—because now I'm stuck in a situation where I can't add anything else to it without bringing render times to a snail's pace. It sucks.

Despite its problematic status today, however, I still love my raytracer. The lifelike images it generates give me a feeling of satisfaction I have yet to observe anywhere else.

Special thanks to my old friend @Speedeo (bitmaps), and more recently @Vurv (pnglib) for helping me encode my traces into these mostly-to-spec images.
